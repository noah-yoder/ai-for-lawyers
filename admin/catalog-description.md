## Full description (~150 words)

Generative AI is changing how law is practiced, and ABA Formal Opinion 512 (2024) makes competence with these tools an ethical obligation — not an elective skill. This course pairs the legal landscape governing AI with hands-on training in using AI for legal work. Each weekly session combines a doctrinal segment — AI regulation, privacy and data protection, IP, litigation and evidentiary issues, liability, algorithmic bias — with a skills lab in which students use AI tools for legal research, drafting, document review, and analysis, with disciplined verification. Every lab is anchored to the professional-responsibility duty it implicates: competence, confidentiality, supervision, fees, or candor. Students complete a semester-long capstone: designing, stress-testing, and defending an AI-assisted legal workflow for a realistic practice scenario, culminating in a written memo and live demonstration. Exercises run on free, consumer-grade tools; professional legal AI platforms are demonstrated where they meaningfully differ. Laptop required.

## Short description (~50 words)

Hands-on training in practicing law with AI, paired with the doctrine that governs it. Weekly skills labs in AI-assisted research, drafting, and review — each anchored to an ethics duty under ABA Formal Opinion 512 — plus a semester capstone designing and defending an AI-assisted legal workflow. Laptop required.
