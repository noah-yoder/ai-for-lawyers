# Before Week 1: Set Up Your AI Accounts (15 Minutes, Required)

*AI for Lawyers — IU McKinney. Send/post this to enrolled students at least one week before the first class.*

Welcome to AI for Lawyers. Your first lab starts hands-on in week one — you'll run the same legal task through three different AI tools and watch them disagree. That only works if your accounts already exist when you walk in. We budget four minutes of class time for setup problems, not forty, so do the following before Week 1. It takes about fifteen minutes.

## 1. Create a free ChatGPT account

Go to **chatgpt.com** and sign up for a free account (no paid tier needed — ever, for this course). Then log out, log back in, and send the model any test message to confirm the account works end to end.

## 2. Create a free Claude account

Go to **claude.ai** and do the same: sign up for the free tier, verify you can log in, and send one test message.

## 3. Set up NotebookLM on a *personal* Google account

This is the step people get wrong, so read it twice:

- Use a **personal** Google account (gmail.com or similar) — **not** your IU account. University Google Workspace policies may block NotebookLM, and you won't find out until mid-lab. If you don't have a personal Google account, create one free at accounts.google.com.
- Signed in to that personal account, go to **notebooklm.google.com** and confirm two things: the page opens, and you can click **"Create new"** to start a notebook. (You can delete the empty notebook afterward.)

## 4. Test all three on campus Wi-Fi, on your lab laptop

Before the first class, take the laptop you'll bring to lab, connect to the classroom/campus Wi-Fi, and confirm you can log in to all three tools — chatgpt.com, claude.ai, and notebooklm.google.com. Home Wi-Fi doesn't count; campus networks occasionally behave differently, and Week 1 is the wrong time to discover that.

If any of the three won't load or won't log you in on campus, email the instructors before the first class so we can sort it out — don't wait for lab day.

## 5. Bring that laptop to every lab

Every weekly lab is hands-on, on your own machine. Bring the laptop you tested, charged, to every session. No laptop, no lab.

## 6. The house rule (starting now)

**Nothing real ever goes into these tools.** No real client facts, no employer information, no details of your own or anyone else's legal matters — not in this course, not while testing your accounts. Everything you'll work on is fictional and provided by us. Why this rule exists is a whole week of the course (Week 4); for now, just follow it absolutely.

---

That's it. Three free accounts, one Wi-Fi test, one rule. See you in Week 1.
