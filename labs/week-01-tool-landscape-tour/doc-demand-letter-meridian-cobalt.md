**Re: Demand for Payment — Destroyed and Damaged Cargo; Master Transportation Agreement dated October 1, 2024 (the "MTA")**

Dear Counsel:

This firm represents Meridian Gearworks LLC ("Meridian"), a precision machining company headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. We write regarding the catastrophic loss your company caused on January 22, 2026, while transporting Meridian's cargo, and to demand payment as set out below.

**Background.** Under the MTA, Cobalt Harbor Logistics, Inc. ("Cobalt Harbor") agreed to provide dedicated freight carriage for Meridian's outbound equipment shipments. On January 20, 2026, Meridian tendered five precision CNC grinding machines (Meridian Model GX-7) to Cobalt Harbor at Meridian's Indianapolis facility for delivery to Meridian's customer, Sycamore Bend Aerospace LLC, in Evansville, Indiana. Each machine was crated, tagged, and accompanied by handling instructions that Meridian's shipping manager, Curtis Almeida, reviewed with your driver at pickup.

On January 22, 2026, on Interstate 69 near Anderson, Indiana, the trailer carrying Meridian's machines left the roadway. The Indiana State Police crash report attributes the incident to a shifted load. Photographs taken at the scene — which we will produce upon request — show that the crating straps were never anchored to the trailer's rated tie-down points, in direct violation of the handling instructions and of Section 4 of the MTA, under which Cobalt Harbor agreed to "exercise the highest degree of care in the loading, securement, and carriage of Shipper's goods."

Two of the five machines were destroyed. A third sustained spindle and gantry damage requiring complete rebuilds. Because Meridian could not deliver on schedule, Sycamore Bend Aerospace terminated its purchase order with Meridian on February 6, 2026.

**Meridian's Losses.** Meridian's damages are as follows:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Two GX-7 machines, destroyed (replacement cost) | $310,000 |
| Rebuild of damaged third machine | $45,000 |
| Lost Sycamore Bend Aerospace purchase order | $100,000 |
| **Total demanded** | **$480,000** |

**Liability.** Cobalt Harbor's liability here is not reasonably debatable. Your driver failed to secure a load of obvious value and fragility, despite written handling instructions and an in-person walkthrough. Section 4 of the MTA imposes the highest standard of care, and the State Police report establishes the breach.

We anticipate your office may point to Section 7 of the MTA. Be advised that, notwithstanding any purported limitation of liability, such provisions are unenforceable against a carrier's gross negligence under Indiana law. *See Hoosier Freightways, Inc. v. Brandt Tooling Co.*, 198 N.E.3d 9006 (Ind. Ct. App. 2024) (refusing to enforce contractual damages cap where carrier disregarded shipper's written securement instructions).

**Demand.** Meridian demands payment of **$480,000** within fourteen (14) days of the date of this letter, and in no event later than **March 20, 2026**. If payment is not received by that date, Meridian has authorized this firm to file suit in the Marion County Superior Court without further notice, where Meridian will seek its full damages, prejudgment interest, and all other available relief.

This letter is written without prejudice to Meridian's rights and remedies, all of which are expressly reserved. Nothing herein shall be construed as a waiver of any kind.
