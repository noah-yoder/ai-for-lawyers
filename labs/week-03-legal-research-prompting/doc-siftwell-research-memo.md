## I. Question Presented

Whether Sablewood Talent Systems, Inc. ("Sablewood"), a Delaware corporation headquartered in Indianapolis with no EU establishment, is subject to Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the "EU AI Act") with respect to SiftWell, its AI-powered applicant-screening platform licensed to employers in Germany and the Netherlands.

## II. Short Answer

Yes, almost certainly — and the exposure may be worse than a compliance burden. SiftWell appears to fall within the Act's extraterritorial scope and its high-risk category for employment tools, and one provision may prohibit the product's core ranking function outright. A possible small-provider exemption and the absence of US enforcement exposure are discussed below.

## III. Analysis

### A. Territorial scope

The AI Act applies to providers placing AI systems on the market or putting them into service in the Union "irrespective of whether those providers are established or located within the Union or in a third country," and separately to providers and deployers located in a third country "where the output produced by the AI system is used in the Union." **[Cite 1: Reg. (EU) 2024/1689, Art. 2(1)(a), (c).]** Sablewood licenses SiftWell to Falkenrath Logistik GmbH (Germany) and Vondelmeer Retail B.V. (Netherlands), whose HR teams use SiftWell's candidate rankings to fill EU-based roles. Both prongs appear satisfied: SiftWell is placed on the EU market, and its output is used in the Union. Sablewood would qualify as a "provider" under the Act.

### B. Risk classification

AI systems "intended to be used for the recruitment or selection of natural persons," including systems used "to analyse and filter job applications, and to evaluate candidates," are classified as high-risk. **[Cite 2: Art. 6(2) in conjunction with Annex III, point 4(a).]** SiftWell screens and ranks applicants for its customers' EU job openings; it falls squarely within this category. High-risk classification triggers the provider obligations summarized in Part III.F below.

### C. Potential prohibited-practice problem

More troubling, Article 5(1)(f) of the Act separately prohibits, as an "unacceptable risk" practice, any AI system that ranks or scores job candidates. This prohibition took effect on February 2, 2025, and violations carry administrative fines of up to €35,000,000 or 7% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher, under Article 99(3). **[Cite 3: Art. 5(1)(f); Art. 99(3).]** Because candidate ranking is SiftWell's core function, the product may be banned outright in the EU regardless of any compliance program. This issue should be elevated immediately.

### D. Possible exemption

There is a potentially significant carve-out. Article 2(14) of the Act exempts providers that have no Union establishment and whose annual turnover in the Union does not exceed €10,000,000. **[Cite 4: Art. 2(14).]** Sablewood's EU-derived revenue was approximately €6.2 million in 2025, so the exemption may apply. Note, however, that the exemption's interaction with the Article 5 prohibitions is unclear.

### E. US enforcement exposure

Domestic exposure appears limited. At least one federal court has held that US vendors of AI tools deployed in the EU face no liability in US courts arising from alleged EU AI Act violations. **[Cite 5: TalentSift Analytics, Inc. v. Bureau of Labor Standards, 412 F. Supp. 4th 882, 889 (N.D. Cal. 2025).]** Accordingly, the regulatory risk is concentrated in the EU.

### F. Key obligations if SiftWell is high-risk (and not prohibited)

If SiftWell is high-risk rather than prohibited, Sablewood's principal obligations as a provider would include: a risk-management system (Art. 9); data-governance requirements (Art. 10); technical documentation (Art. 11); human-oversight design (Art. 14); conformity assessment (Art. 43); registration in the EU database (Art. 49); and, because Sablewood has no EU establishment, the appointment of an EU authorised representative (Art. 22). These obligations apply to Annex III high-risk systems from August 2, 2026.
