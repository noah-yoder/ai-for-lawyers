Counselor —

I'll keep this short because honestly the facts speak for themselves. We're done with Cobalt Harbor Logistics and I want to file against them as soon as possible. As far as I can tell this is as open and shut as it gets.

Background: We're a precision parts manufacturer here in Indianapolis — about 60 employees. In March 2024 we signed a Master Services Agreement with Cobalt Harbor Logistics, Inc. for all our warehousing and outbound freight, effective April 1, 2024. When their sales rep, Tom Garrity, was pitching us, he told me — in my own conference room, with my plant manager sitting right there — that Cobalt Harbor "guarantees two-day delivery to anywhere in the Midwest, period." That guarantee was the whole reason we signed.

Starting in January of this year, their performance fell off a cliff. Between January and April 2026 we had at least nine shipments arrive late — some by four or five days. I called their account rep, Renee Castellano, at least five times to complain. Every time it was "we're on it, won't happen again." I have my own call notes.

Then came the disaster. In April, a late shipment caused us to miss a contractual delivery window with our biggest customer, Sablewood Marine Group. Sablewood canceled a $250,000 purchase order on the spot and told us they're "re-evaluating the relationship." That relationship is worth about $2.4 million a year to us. If we lose Sablewood entirely, that's the company's future.

And here's the kicker: after the Sablewood fiasco, Renee sent me an email saying, quote, "We're very sorry for the inconvenience and we value the relationship." **They admitted fault in writing.** What more do we need?

One more thing, just so you have the full picture — our bookkeeper was out for surgery twice last year, so two of our monthly payments to Cobalt Harbor went out about three weeks late (October and December 2025). They never made a fuss about it and we're fully current now. I assume that's a non-issue.

I want to go after them for the $250,000 lost order, the value of the Sablewood relationship, and frankly whatever else we can get. My brother-in-law (he's in commercial real estate, but still) says we should also demand punitive damages because the two-day guarantee was an outright lie.

When can we file? I'd like to see a complaint by the end of the month.

Dana Whitfield
