# Triage Scenario Cards — Week 4

Six things a junior associate is about to do with an AI tool. For each one, use the Data Triage Guide to assign a tier:

- **GREEN** — fine on a free consumer tool, as-is
- **YELLOW** — usable on a consumer tool only after sanitization
- **RED** — enterprise / firm-approved tool only (or get informed consent first)
- **BLACK** — no AI tool at all, in any tier

Be ready to defend your answer with the three triage questions, not a gut feeling.

---

## Card 1

A partner asks you to summarize a published Seventh Circuit opinion she's citing in a brief. You paste the full text of the opinion from the court's website into free ChatGPT and ask for a one-page summary of the holding and reasoning.

## Card 2

You're reviewing a draft executive employment agreement for a client, Cobalt Harbor Logistics, Inc. To save time, you paste the entire draft — including the executive's name, title, salary, equity grant, and non-compete terms — into your personal free Claude.ai account and ask it to "flag any unusual clauses."

## Card 3

You want help drafting witness-interview questions. Careful about confidentiality, you describe the matter to free ChatGPT only as "a mid-sized logistics company facing a Department of Labor wage-and-hour audit." You don't name anyone. Your firm has exactly one logistics client, and the audit was reported in an industry trade publication last month.

## Card 4

You're prepping for a deposition in an active case that's subject to a stipulated protective order. You upload 200 pages of deposition transcripts — designated CONFIDENTIAL under the order — to NotebookLM on your personal Google account so you can query them while you study.

## Card 5

While working on a confidential client matter, you ask free ChatGPT a pure legal research question: "What are the elements of conversion under Indiana law? Cite the leading cases." You include nothing about the client or the facts.

## Card 6

Your client's general counsel emails you: "Run the demand letter we received through that AI thing and give me a settlement-value estimate — you have my blessing." The demand letter came from opposing counsel, but a useful estimate would require mixing in your client's confidential facts (insurance posture, internal admissions, financials).
